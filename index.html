A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile man of India

Here's a time-line of Dr. Kalam's Life

  • 1931 Born on 15th October in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu .
  • 1954 Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirapalli
  • Enrolled at Madras Institute of Technology to study aerospace engineering
  • 1960 Joined DRDO as Chief Scientist
  • 1965 Started work rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 Transfered to ISRO
  • 1980 Given responsibility for the development of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles
  • 1981 Padma Bhushan Government of India
  • 1990 Padma Vibhushan Government of India
  • 1992-99 Served as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the PM and Secretary of DRDO
  • 1997 Bharat Ratna Government of India
  • 2002-07 Served as 11th President of India
  • 2012 Launched mission for the youth of the nation called "What Can I Give Movement"
  • 2015 Passed away on 27th July in Shillong, Meghalaya

"If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun."

--A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
